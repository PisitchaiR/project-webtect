BORN AT THE PRECIPICE OF THE SOCIAL MEDIA WAVE, 2010, YOMZANSI HAS GAINED TRACTION AS A LEADING PUBLICATION IN THE REALM OF MUSIC, SNEAKERS, FASHION, EVENTS, TECH & MOTORING. FOUNDERS THABO AND THABISO ARE EXPERTS IN THIS SPACE OF INTERSECTING CULTURAL EVENTS, SHOWCASING THE LATEST TRENDS AND NEWS FOR A SOUTH AFRICAN AUDIENCE.

They were one of the first spaces to truly teach us all that we can, and should, take charge of remaining on the global pulse – that the whole concept of the southern hemisphere being “a season behind” (as it has been thought, particularly in fashion) is completely obsolete. YOMZANSI curates an incredibly well balanced array of offerings on both local and international information that informs their community to be autonomous participants in the culture – as it happens. It is with this spirit of independence that we could think of no better collaboration for the release of the Reebok Kamikaze, a high-tech feel sneaker with its signature TV static foam, tempered by the signature franchise colours of teal, red, black and purple. The Kamikaze is one of those silhouettes that is both nostalgic, with a reference to 80s and 90s pop cultural hype, while still feeling classically “futuristic” in its aesthetic. It is the perfect sneaker for this current transitional period in 2021, where we are beginning to fully settle into this new century and our contemporary expression of culture within it.

“WE WERE INTERESTED IN WHAT WE COULD DO THE INTERNET AS A TOOL. OUR VISION IN 2010 WAS TO KEEP PEOPLE POSTED – THE WAY WE WANTED TO, FOR OURSELVES – AND EVERY YEAR SINCE THEN IT HAS BEEN AN ESCALATION AND REFINEMENT OF YOMZANSI AS A BUSINESS. I THINK THE COOLEST PART OF IT HAS BEEN CONNECTING WITH BRANDS, AND CREATING THIS SPACE THAT MERGES MEDIA AND E-COMMERCE.

We wanted to be integrated, like a one-stop-shop, so that our community could keep up with what is happening during their day or evening. As soon as we started connecting with more brands, we realised that our power lies in maintaining this relationship in between launches and collaborations. If we feature a brand on our e-commerce site, it means we are always connected – and the culture continues to be created and stay alive.” Thabiso muses in our conversation, reflecting on the last 11 years.

Our greatest value at YOMZANSI is being guides for our audience. We can look to Highsnobiety, Nice Kicks, Hypebeast and be inspired by them – but we want to do it as Africans, with our own expression. We have so much to give the world, and as much as we learn from those spaces – we need to show what we have. I am really excited to find new ways to interact with our community through digital and physical means. Even in lockdown, we started a sneaker battle and kept the community together during a really uncertain time – I want to grow the potential for everything we can do in that way.

IN REFLECTING ON THE IMAGES FOR THIS COLLABORATION, WE ASKED ABOUT THE VIVID COLOURS AND POP CULTURAL ENERGY THAT WE FELT WHEN WE SAW THEM –

“We really wanted to style this shoot to showcase the Kamikaze and urge people to consider colour for the season ahead. Its colourway is unusual for the current trend – we are seeing a lot of monochrome and minimalism right now – but I love the boldness of the heritage colours, and I was reminded of the 80s and 90s hip-hop era that started it all. The TV static too – it is this reference to vintage technology, but in a very relevant moment.” Thabiso reveals.

LEVEN YEARS IN, IN THE WORLD OF MEDIA TODAY, IS THE BEGINNING OF TRUE LEGACY BUILDING. TO HOLD A SPACE FOR THAT LONG DURING THE RISE AND FALL OF PRINT AND DIGITAL MEDIA, AND STILL HAVE THE PASSION AND MOTIVATION TO CHALLENGE LIMITATIONS AND BREAK BOUNDARIES – WE ASK THABISO WHAT HIS PERSONAL VISION IS FOR THE 2022 –

“I want to keep learning, man. I learn every day, and I think we live in such a crazy time – yes, it’s scary at times – but ultimately, we are a generation that is living firmly in the future. What happens in the next decade is going to be unexpected, and I want to remain present for that.”
